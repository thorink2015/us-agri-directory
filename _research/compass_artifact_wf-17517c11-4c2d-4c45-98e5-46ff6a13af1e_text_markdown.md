## SECTION 1 — TOP 5 ANCHOR STATS (for article intro)

These are the highest-impact, most defensible, most recent figures. Use 2-3 in the lede.

1. **US ag spray drones treated 16.4 million acres in 2025, up 58.7% year-over-year from 10.3 million in 2024 — and roughly 4.4× the 3.7 million acres sprayed in 2023.** Source: American Spray Drone Coalition (ASDC) 2025 Impact Survey, published January 2026. ASDC member distributors represent ~80% of the US ag spray drone market. **Tier 1.**

2. **The FAA had certificated 1,710 Part 137 UAS operators by September 2025, a 58.3% jump in one year and a number that effectively did not exist in 2020.** Source: FAA *Safety Briefing* (Sept/Oct 2025), cited in ASDC Impact Survey; corroborated by FAA BVLOS NPRM (Docket FAA-2025-1908, Aug 2025), which references "over 1,700" cumulative ag UAS operator certificates. **Tier 1.**

3. **The average US drone spray service charged $13/acre in 2025, down 38% from $21/acre in 2024 — a price collapse driven by rapid operator entry and discounting by non-Part-137 actors.** Source: ASDC 2025 Impact Survey. **Tier 1.**

4. **93.5% of agricultural spray drones sold in the United States in 2024 were Chinese-made (predominantly DJI Agras). That share fell to 75.75% in 2025 after CBP's UFLPA enforcement and the FCC's December 22, 2025 Covered List ruling.** Source: ASDC Comments to Department of Commerce BIS (filed via reginfo.gov, Dec 19, 2025). **Tier 1.**

5. **The US still trails China by roughly 26× in drone-treated acreage. China's ag drones treated approximately 173 million hectares (~427 million acres) in 2024 — about one-third of all Chinese cropland — versus 16.4 million acres in the US.** Source: DJI/Farmers' Daily White Paper on the Agricultural Drone Industry (2024/2025), reported via *China Daily*, Aug 27, 2025. **Tier 2 (vendor white paper, methodology partially disclosed).**

## SECTION 3 — KEY TAKEAWAYS (8-12 BULLETS, FOR ARTICLE)

- 16.4M acres treated by spray drones in the US in 2025 — a 58.7% YoY jump.
- 1,710 FAA Part 137 UAS operator certificates issued by Sept 2025, up 58.3% YoY.
- $215M rural economic activity generated by spray drone services in 2024 (ASDC).
- Average US per-acre spray drone price fell from $21 (2024) to $13 (2025) — a 38% one-year drop.
- 93.5% of ag spray drones sold in the US in 2024 were Chinese-made; that fell to 75.75% in 2025.
- New unit sales fell 59% in 2025 (8,950 → 3,711) due to CBP/UFLPA and FCC enforcement.
- 13% of NAAA-surveyed manned aerial operators reported using drones in 2025 (vs 5% in 2024).
- 27% of US ag retailers offer drone-applied crop inputs (CropLife/Purdue 2025), down from 35% in 2024.
- 22% of Iowa farmers used a drone or drone service in 2024 (Iowa Farm and Rural Life Poll 2025).
- Peer-reviewed studies show drone spraying cuts pesticide use 46–75% and drift 65–70% versus conventional ground/airblast.
- China leads global ag drone use ~26× the US, with ~173M hectares treated in 2024.
- FAA's Part 108 BVLOS rule (final expected Spring 2026) and the FCC Covered List (Dec 22, 2025) are the two regulatory pivots reshaping the 2026–2027 market.

## SECTION 8 — METHODOLOGY CAVEATS (FOR ARTICLE'S METHODOLOGY SECTION)

1. **NAAA membership ≠ industry headcount.** NAAA had 1,391 total members (Sept 2025) but estimates 1,560 manned operators + 2,028 manned ag pilots + 1,082 Part 137 drone operators = ~4,670 industry persons. The 1,082 drone-operator figure is NAAA's count of FAA-issued certificates, not its members.

2. **CropLife/Purdue is a dealer survey, not a farmer survey.** Statements like "X% of farmers use drones" cannot be derived from it. The 2025 survey sampled 93 ag retail input suppliers, mostly Midwest field-crop dealers.

3. **ASDC has an advocacy posture** (formed in 2024 to oppose Chinese-drone bans). Acreage and economic figures are derived from member-distributor surveys covering ~80% of the US ag spray drone market — best available, but methodology should be disclosed in the article. The Dec 19, 2025 BIS submission and Jan 2026 Impact Survey are the freshest, most-cited primary documents.

4. **"American Spray Drone Coalition" and "Aerial Spray Drone Coalition"** are the same organization (ASDC). Used both names in different filings.

5. **NAAA 2025 trend caveat — substitution effect.** Manned ag aviation showed declines (avg acres per operation down 17% YoY; 49% of operators flew fewer acres) in the same year drone-using operations rose from 5% to 13%. This points to substitution, not pure additive growth.

6. **USDA NASS does NOT yet break out drone application as a distinct precision-ag category** in the 2022 Census of Agriculture. This is itself a finding worth flagging in the article: official federal statistics lag the operational reality by 3-5 years.

7. **State pesticide-applicator licensing** for drone operators is fragmented. California (AB 1016) created a private-applicator UAV pesticide certificate. Most states fold drone applications into existing aerial categories. There is no consolidated cross-state count of drone-specific applicators.

8. **FAA does not appear to publish year-end Part 137 holder counts as a public time series.** The Sept 2025 figure (1,710) is via FAA Safety Briefing; the cumulative 1,700+ is from the Aug 2025 BVLOS NPRM. Year-by-year reconstructions for 2020-2024 require FOIA.

## SECTION 11 — NOTES TO THE ARTICLE WRITER

- **The 2024–2025 inflection is real and quantifiable.** Treated acreage roughly tripled from 3.7M (2023) to 10.3M (2024) to 16.4M (2025). Part 137 UAS operators grew 58% YoY to 1,710. This is the article's central narrative arc.

- **The market is mid-shock.** New unit sales fell 59% in 2025 due to UFLPA enforcement and the FCC Covered List, even as treated acreage grew 59%. That paradox — more flying with fewer new drones — is a strong sub-story. Existing fleets are flying more; price competition is fierce.

- **Avoid the "X% of farmers use drones" trap.** No primary source supports a single such figure. The cleanest farmer-level proxy is Iowa's 22% (Iowa Farm Poll 2025). Use it carefully; don't generalize.

- **The market-size cross-reference is genuinely useful editorial content.** Lead with Grand View's $506M (2024) US-specific figure as the most defensible. Note the global spread of $1.5B–$7.9B (2025) and 8%–33% CAGRs as a feature of an immature market.

- **The DJI political story is active.** As of April 2026: FCC Covered List effective Dec 22, 2025; DJI sued in 9th Circuit Feb 20, 2026; FCC waiver for Blue UAS through Jan 1, 2027; FAA Part 108 BVLOS final rule expected Spring 2026. Frame as "fluid regulatory environment" and date-stamp specific claims.

- **Environmental stats are the strongest peer-reviewed material in this corpus.** Lead with the Nature meta-review (46–75% pesticide reduction), the ACS study (90–99% operator exposure reduction), and the PLOS ONE LCA (71.8% water reduction). These are bulletproof.

- **Geographic story = Mid-South delta + California specialty crops + emerging Corn Belt.** Rice, cotton, soybean (Mississippi/Arkansas/Louisiana) and vineyards/orchards (California) are the established markets. The Corn Belt is the fastest-growing but still secondary.

- **Operator economics are tightening.** Per-acre price fell 38% in one year while average acres-per-operator stayed flat at 9,584. Many new entrants will exit. Guardian Agriculture's August 2025 collapse despite $51.7M raised illustrates execution risk. Hylio's path ($30M+ revenue, 850+ drones, scaling to 5,000/yr) is the survivor template.

- **Total verified stat count delivered: ~85 (master list) plus an additional ~30 contextual/secondary figures. After filtering for the article's 40-60 final stats, prioritize Tier 1 ASDC, NAAA, FAA, peer-reviewed, and university extension over Tier 2 aggregators.**
