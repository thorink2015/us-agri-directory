# California Agricultural Drone Operator Directory (Batch 5a)

## Summary

**Total entities found: 51** across all categories. California's agricultural drone spraying market remains nascent despite being the largest US agricultural state. Complex overlapping regulations (FAA Part 137, CDPR Unmanned Pest Control Aircraft Pilot Certificate, county Agricultural Commissioner registration) have slowed adoption compared to Midwestern states. AB 1016, signed by Gov. Newsom in October 2023, is accelerating growth but the operator count remains well below the 100+ initially expected.

- Spray/application operators (commercial): 13 active, 1 defunct (Guardian Agriculture)
- Biocontrol/pollination drone operators: 2
- Scouting/mapping operators: 2
- Individual operator/farmer-operators: 3

- Training schools and programs: 11
- Manufacturers (CA-based or CA office): 6
- Platforms/marketplaces: 2

**Key data gaps:** No public FAA database of Part 137 UAS certificate holders exists. CDPR publishes licensee CSV files but does not distinguish manned from unmanned aircraft pilots. Many operators lack public websites and were found only through news articles, job postings, or regulatory filings. Social media presence is sparse for most CA ag drone operators. The market is heavily concentrated in the Salinas Valley, Central Valley, and Southern California; Napa/Sonoma wine country has scouting operators but very few spray operators. Several entities (Specialty Drone Solutions, Scout AI) have limited independent verification.

## California regulatory notes

**FAA requirements for agricultural drone spraying in California:**

Federal law requires a layered certification stack. Pilots must hold an **FAA Part 107 Remote Pilot Certificate** for all commercial UAS operations. For agricultural spraying with drones, operators must additionally obtain an **FAA Part 137 Agricultural Aircraft Operator Certificate** (Form 8710-3). Drones over 55 lbs require a **Section 44807 exemption** (formerly Section 333). The FAA has streamlined the process through a dedicated UAS137Certificates@faa.gov intake. No public searchable FAA database of Part 137 UAS certificate holders exists; operators must be identified through regulations.gov dockets or self-disclosure.

**California Department of Pesticide Regulation (CDPR) requirements:**

California imposes state-level licensing beyond federal FAA requirements. AB 527 (now AB 1016, signed by Gov. Newsom October 2023) established the **Unmanned Pest Control Aircraft Pilot Certificate**, administered by the CDPR Director. Three certificate types exist: UAS Apprentice, UAS Journeyman, and Vector Control Technician. Operators applying restricted-use pesticides must also hold a **Qualified Applicator License (QAL)** or **Qualified Applicator Certificate (QAC)**. CDPR publishes licensee CSV files at https://apps.cdpr.ca.gov/docs/license/currlic.cfm but these do not distinguish between manned and unmanned aircraft pilots. **County Agricultural Commissioner registration:**

Operators must register annually with the **County Agricultural Commissioner** in each county where they intend to operate. Fees vary by county. This is a separate registration from CDPR and FAA licensing. Agricultural use reports (PUR) must be filed with the commissioner after each application.

**Key regulatory context:**

AB 1016 significantly simplified drone spray pilot licensing effective 2024, but the multi-layer requirements (Part 107 + Part 137 + 44807 exemption + CDPR certificate + QAL/QAC + county registration) still present a higher regulatory barrier than most US states. This explains why California has fewer active ag drone spray operators than states like Iowa, Kansas, or Nebraska despite being the nation's largest agricultural state by revenue. President Trump's June 2025 executive order accelerating BVLOS permissions may further stimulate California adoption. The California Agricultural Aircraft Association (CAAA), based in Lincoln CA, represents approximately 90% of the state's aerial pest control operators but remains primarily focused on manned aircraft. NAAA's Uncrewed Aerial Application Systems Committee published educational flyers for spray drone operators in March 2026.
