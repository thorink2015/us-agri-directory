## Page 4: `/glossary`

**Title:** `Agricultural Drone Glossary | 60+ Terms Defined | Ag Drone Directory`
**Meta:** `Definitions for agricultural drone terms: Part 107, Part 137, NDAA, gpa, VT/R1, NDVI, RTK, and 55+ more. Plain-language explanations for farmers and operators.`
**H1:** `Agricultural Drone Glossary`

### AEO Block
This glossary defines 60+ terms used in agricultural drone spraying, including FAA certifications (Part 107, Part 137, Section 44807), pesticide application terms (gpa, REI, PHI, buffer zone), crop growth stages (VT/R1, R2/R3, T3), drone technology (RTK, NDVI, NDRE, LiDAR), and industry acronyms (NDAA, NAAA, NRCS, EQIP).

### Format
Alphabetical list. Each term: bold term name, 1 to 3 sentence plain-language definition. Link to the relevant page on this site where applicable.

### Terms (minimum 60, grouped alphabetically)

**A**
- **Aerial applicator** — A person or business that applies pesticides, fertilizer, or seed from an aircraft, including drones. Requires FAA Part 137 and a state commercial pesticide applicator license.
- **AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)** — Structuring web content so AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) can cite it directly.
- **APHIS** — USDA Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service. Coordinates emergency pest response and quarantine enforcement.

**B**
- **Buffer zone** — A required setback distance between the spray application area and sensitive sites (water bodies, schools, residences, neighboring crops). Set by product label and sometimes by state regulation.
- **BVLOS (Beyond Visual Line of Sight)** — Flying a drone beyond the pilot's direct visual range. Requires an FAA waiver under Part 107.

**C**
- **Carrier volume** — The amount of liquid (water plus chemical) applied per acre, measured in gallons per acre (gpa). Drones typically apply 2 to 5 gpa versus 15 to 20 for ground rigs.
- **CE / CEU (Continuing Education / Continuing Education Unit)** — Credits required to maintain state pesticide applicator certification. Requirements vary by state from 4 to 24 credits per cycle.
- **Cover crop** — A crop planted primarily to protect and improve soil rather than for harvest. Cereal rye, crimson clover, and annual ryegrass are the most common species seeded by drone.
- **CSP** — Conservation Stewardship Program. USDA program that pays for conservation performance including precision application practices.

**D**
- **Defoliant** — A chemical that removes leaves from a crop before harvest. Commonly applied to cotton by drone in September and October.
- **DON (Deoxynivalenol)** — A mycotoxin produced by Fusarium head blight in wheat. Proper fungicide timing reduces DON by 40 to 60 percent.
- **Drift** — Movement of spray droplets away from the target area. The primary environmental and legal risk for aerial applicators.
- **DPL (Drone Pilot License)** — Arizona-specific credential for drone pesticide application, separate from manned aircraft pilot license.

**E**
- **EQIP** — Environmental Quality Incentives Program. USDA NRCS cost-share program that covers drone equipment purchases (Practice Code 595) and cover crop seeding (Practice Standard 340).

**F**
- **FAA** — Federal Aviation Administration. Governs drone flight rules through Part 107 (pilot) and Part 137 (agricultural aircraft operations).
- **FIFRA** — Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act. The federal law governing pesticide registration, labeling, and use. All drone spray operations must comply with FIFRA product labels.

**G**
- **GPA (gallons per acre)** — The standard unit for carrier volume in aerial application. Drones typically apply 2 to 5 gpa.

**H**
- **Hull insurance** — Insurance covering physical damage to the drone itself from crashes, weather, or fire. Typically $500 to $2,000 per year per drone.

**I**
- **IP rating** — Ingress Protection rating measuring dust and water resistance. IP55 means protected from dust and low-pressure water jets. IPX6K (XAG P100 Pro) means protected from high-pressure water.

**L**
- **LiDAR** — Light Detection and Ranging. Used on spray drones for terrain-following and obstacle avoidance.

**M**
- **MTOW (Maximum Takeoff Weight)** — The maximum weight of the drone including payload, battery, and fuel. Determines FAA regulatory category (over 55 lbs requires Section 44807 exemption).

**N**
- **NAAA** — National Agricultural Aviation Association. Trade organization for aerial applicators including drone operators.
- **NDAA** — National Defense Authorization Act. Restricts federal agencies from using drones from designated foreign manufacturers (DJI, XAG). Does not restrict private commercial use.
- **NDRE (Normalized Difference Red Edge)** — A vegetation index using the red edge band. Better than NDVI for detecting stress in dense canopy crops like mature corn.
- **NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index)** — A spectral index measuring plant health from drone imagery. High NDVI = healthy dense biomass.
- **NRCS** — Natural Resources Conservation Service. USDA agency that administers EQIP and other conservation programs.

**P**
- **Part 107** — FAA regulation governing small UAS operations. The baseline pilot certification for all commercial drone flights. 60-question exam, $175, 70 percent passing.
- **Part 137** — FAA regulation governing agricultural aircraft operations. Required for any commercial aerial application of pesticides, including by drone.
- **PHI (Preharvest Interval)** — The minimum number of days between the last pesticide application and crop harvest. Set by the product label.
- **Pollution liability** — Insurance covering pesticide drift damage, water contamination, and environmental claims. Separate from standard drone liability insurance.

**R**
- **RCPP** — Regional Conservation Partnership Program. USDA program that can layer additional cost-share on top of EQIP for cover crop and conservation practices.
- **REI (Restricted Entry Interval)** — The minimum time after pesticide application before workers can re-enter the treated area without PPE. Set by the product label.
- **Remote ID** — FAA requirement (effective September 2023) for drones to broadcast identification and location information during flight.
- **RTK (Real-Time Kinematic)** — GPS correction technology providing centimeter-level positioning accuracy. Used on spray drones for precise flight paths with under 3 percent overlap.
- **RUP (Restricted Use Pesticide)** — A pesticide classified by EPA as requiring additional protections. Application requires a certified applicator, and some states require additional aerial permits for RUP application by drone.

**S**
- **Section 44807** — FAA authorization required for commercial operation of drones over 55 lbs. Filed as part of the Part 137 application for most ag spray drones.
- **Swath width** — The width of the spray pattern on each pass. Typically 4 to 13 meters (13 to 43 feet) depending on drone model and nozzle configuration.

**T**
- **T3 heading** — Wheat growth stage when heads emerge from the boot. The critical fungicide application window for Fusarium head blight control.

**U**
- **UAS (Unmanned Aircraft System)** — The complete system including the drone, controller, and associated equipment. The FAA term for what is commonly called a "drone."

**V**
- **VT/R1** — Corn growth stages: VT is tassel emergence, R1 is silking. The critical fungicide application window for corn, typically mid-to-late July in the Corn Belt.
